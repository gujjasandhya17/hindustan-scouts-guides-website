About HSGA

The Hindustan Scouts and Guides Association (HSGA) was established in 1928 by the renowned social figure, freedom fighter, and Bharat Ratna Mahamana Pandit Madan Mohan Malviya, inspired by Pandit Sri Ram Ajpei to serve deprived Hindustani students during British rule.

Over the decades, HSGA has evolved into a premier organization dedicated to character development, leadership training, and fostering a sense of social responsibility among youth across India.

Our mission is to nurture young minds by fostering strong citizenship, equipping them with practical life skills, and inspiring respect for national heritage.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To empower youth across India to become responsible, disciplined, and socially committed citizens by nurturing strong character, fostering leadership, promoting community service, encouraging self-reliance and outdoor skills, translating indigenous practices and knowledge into action for sustainable development.

Our Mission

To nurture young minds by fostering citizenship and social responsibility, equipping them with practical skills such as survival techniques, first aid, and environmental stewardship while emphasizing character development and leadership. We also inspire patriotism, promote digital literacy and AI awareness, and unify regional Scout associations under one cohesive platform.
