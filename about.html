About Us

Our History

The Foundation (1928)

The Hindustan Scouts and Guides Association (HSGA) is rooted in a vision for national regeneration. It was established in 1928 by the stalwart social figure, freedom fighter, and internationally renowned Bharat Ratna Mahamana Pandit Madan Mohan Malviya ji, inspired by Pandit Sri Ram Ajpei.

On Mahamanaji's call, distinguished personalities like Mrs. Annie Besant, Shri Hriday Narayan Kunjiru, and Justice Vibian Bose contributed significantly to the organization's founding and growth.

Historical Significance

While Scouting began globally under Robert Baden-Powell, few know that Powell drew inspiration from the Gurukkul educational system of India. The system's emphasis on character building and practical skills influenced the very foundation of modern Scouting.

Baden-Powell himself acknowledged the contributions of Indian scouts and recognized the extensive knowledge and training systems that existed in India long before organized Scouting began in Britain.

Historical Background

The history of Scouting globally traces back to Robert Baden-Powell, but many are unaware that Powell drew inspiration from the Gurukkul educational system of India. The Gurukkul method of education had been systematically training young people in practical knowledge, survival techniques, and character development for centuries.

After the British established control in India, Captain Seshell served as Baden-Powell's senior scout unit commander. In the 1880s, Baden-Powell came to India where a Scout unit was established within the military department for intelligence purposes. These scouts had exceptional skills in acquiring information, reconnaissance, and tactical operations.

The success and effectiveness of Indian scouts impressed Baden-Powell. After witnessing the potential of well-trained armed forces during the Boer War in Africa, Powell realized that introducing basic military and survival training to European children could be beneficial. This led to the formalization of Scouting as an organized movement.

Upon his retirement, Baden-Powell developed the complete Scouting curriculum. The ideals and practical skills were adapted from the Indian Gurukkul system. Scouting started in Britain before expanding to India, initially serving only European and Anglo-Indian children.

Pandit Sri Ram Bajpayee was instrumental in recognizing Scouting as the English version of the Gurukkul sarpaath-charyakaram. After consulting with Mahamana Madan Mohan Malviya, he translated Baden-Powell's curriculum into Indian languages and integrated indigenous knowledge systems, creating "Mamoma Kod" - a sacred symbolic script that unified Indian Scouting under one philosophy.

Under Mahamana's guidance, various Scouting institutions across India were unified in 1928 to establish the Hindustan Scouts & Guides Association, dedicated to providing comprehensive education and training to Indian children across the nation.

Recognition and Evolution

International Recognition

Baden-Powell himself acknowledged the exceptional skills of Indian scouts. In 1928, at an official ceremony, after witnessing the capabilities of HSGA scouts, Baden-Powell recommended to the British Viceroy that HSGA's training and certification should be recognized internationally.

Post-Independence Growth

After Indian independence, the assets and organization of British Scouting in India were transferred to the Indian government. The Boy Scouts and Girl Guides (BGS) was established in 1950 under Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru's guidance, while HSGA continued as a parallel organization dedicated to indigenous Indian values and traditional training methods.

Modern Era (1998 - Present)

1998: Under the enthusiastic support of then Prime Minister Shri Atal Bihari Vajpayee and Cabinet Minister of Sports Smt. Uma Bharati, HSGA received formal registration as a society (Reg. S-33939/1998). Shri Sahib Singh Verma, then Chief Minister of Delhi, served as the Founder President until 2006.

2006-2013: Leadership passed to Shri Raman Bora and subsequently Shri Narsingh Mengji as National Presidents, continuing the organization's mission of youth development and social responsibility.

2013-2023: Mr. Purushotam Rupalla served as National President, expanding HSGA's reach and modernizing its programs while maintaining its core values of character development and patriotism.

2023-2026: Following triennial elections held on October 2, 2023, at Kashi Hindu Vishwavidyalaya, HSGA entered a new phase with Dr. Karunakar Pradhan as National Chairman and Shri Girish Juyal as National Secretary. The 65-member executive committee is dedicated to advancing HSGA's mission with renewed vigor and modern approaches.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To empower youth across India to become responsible, disciplined, and socially committed citizens by nurturing strong character, fostering leadership, promoting community service, and encouraging self-reliance and outdoor skills. We are committed to translating indigenous practices and knowledge into action for sustainable development and building a stronger nation.

Our Mission

Core Objectives:

  • Nurture young minds through fostering citizenship and social responsibility
  • Equip youth with practical life skills (survival, first aid, environmental stewardship)
  • Emphasize character development, leadership, and teamwork
  • Inspire respect for national heroes and cultivate patriotism
  • Promote digital literacy and AI awareness
  • Unify regional associations under one cohesive platform
